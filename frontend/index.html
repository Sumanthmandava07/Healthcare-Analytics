Ethical Data Handling & HIPAA/GDPR Compliance

How the Aegis platform protects patients, handles sensitive physiological parameters, and minimizes algorithmic bias.

Data Minimization & In-Memory Calculations

Aegis enforces the principle of data minimization by processing patient physiological metrics completely in-memory. The raw values are never stored in log files, cached on disks, or transmitted to any external databases. Once prediction coefficients are evaluated, the data is permanently flushed from local memory variables.

Pseudonymization & Cryptographic Salting

To adhere to HIPAA Safe Harbor guidelines and GDPR Article 25 requirements, any patient identifying labels (like names or registration IDs) are cryptographically de-identified before calculations. We apply SHA-256 hashes client-side, ensuring that no cleartext Personal Health Information (PHI) is ever exposed in downstream server components.

Algorithmic Bias & Feature Exclusions

Classification algorithms can easily replicate systemic racial, economic, or demographic disparities. Our training datasets (Cleveland Heart Clinic, Pima Indian Communities) are analyzed for demographic representation. We exclude high-bias parameters such as socioeconomic status, ZIP codes, and insurance categories to prevent surrogate demographic profiling, focusing strictly on objective biometrics.
